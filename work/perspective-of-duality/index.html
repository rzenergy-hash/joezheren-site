Work / Perspective of Duality

Perspective of Duality

In this series, decorative pattern becomes a mask. I build aesthetic surfaces — dense, ornamental, often drawn from traditions like Arabic pattern-work — that hold something less comfortable underneath: violence, consumption, or decay pressed just beneath a beautiful skin.

The titles point to this directly — Underneath, Behind Pattern — naming the gap between what a surface shows and what it's covering. Beauty here is not innocent; it's a method, a way that culture and image-making conceal what they'd rather not show plainly. I'm interested in the moment a viewer notices the seam — when the pattern stops being only decorative and starts to look like a deliberate cover, asking what kind of looking it would take to see past it.
