Work / Among the Man-Made

Among the Man-Made

This series asks how our growing entanglement with technology reshapes our sense of what's real. I work in the space between stillness and movement, between the world as given and the world as constructed, to explore how algorithms and virtual environments increasingly mediate our experience of reality.

Each video loops — brief, repeating, easy to miss and hard to forget. The looping itself becomes part of the meaning: a glitch in time that mirrors the recursive, self-reinforcing logic of the digital systems we live inside. Working across video, interactive environments, and virtual game-space, I treat the digital landscape as a new kind of nature — one we've built, but no longer fully control — and ask what it means to locate ourselves within it.

I am drawn to the contradiction between appearance and substance, and between theory and practice. Rather than simply reflecting the polarization of our society, social media help to create it — addicting us in order to maximize profit. Here I explore the tension between the digital and the real, focusing on how artificial intelligence and social media shape our perception of truth. In a world where fake news and digital constructs blur the line between reality and illusion, this work reflects on what these changes do to humanity and culture, questioning the dualities that run through our lives: good and bad, truth and fault, faith and self-doubt.
